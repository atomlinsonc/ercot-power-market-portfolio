# Week 8: Final Reflection and Next Steps

Date: 2026-07
Category: Weekly Reflection

## What I built

I built the first version of an ERCOT Real-Time Market Dashboard using Python, pandas, Streamlit, Plotly, and mock ERCOT-style data. I paired the dashboard with documentation, blog posts, a sample market brief, and a two-month build plan.

## What I learned

The project showed me that market analytics work is not only about charts. It is about building a repeatable process for collecting data, validating fields, summarizing changes, and asking better questions.

## What I still do not understand

I need deeper understanding of ERCOT data products, settlement point naming, congestion, ancillary services, scarcity pricing, and how different desks prioritize day-ahead versus real-time signals.

## What I would ask a power trader

- Which dashboard metric would you want first thing in the morning?
- Which price movements are noise, and which deserve immediate attention?
- How do you decide whether DA/RT spread behavior is meaningful?
- What makes a junior analyst's daily brief useful?

## Next project

Project 2 will focus on Day-Ahead vs Real-Time Spread Analysis and will go deeper on spread distributions, ranked intervals, and possible market explanations.
