# Project 6: ERCOT Market Rules Tracker

Date: July 2026

Category: ERCOT Learning Notes

## What I Worked On

I created a structured ERCOT market-rules tracker for selected NPRRs and related rule-change research. The tracker includes rule ID, title, status, next step, topic, market impact area, priority, source URL, last-checked date, and analyst notes.

## Why It Matters for a Trading Desk

Market data does not exist in isolation. Rule changes can affect how products are defined, how resources participate, how scarcity is interpreted, and which metrics belong in a market brief. A support analyst needs a repeatable way to watch rule changes without pretending to be a rules expert.

## What I Learned About ERCOT

The initial takeaway is that rule tracking is a workflow problem as much as a research problem. The value is not just reading a document; it is classifying the topic, identifying who might care, and turning the item into a useful follow-up question.

## What I Would Improve Next

The next version should add automated stale-date checks, expand beyond the first starter items, and create a weekly rules digest. Any public claim about a rule's status should be verified against ERCOT source pages before publication.

## Resume Relevance

This project shows market research, documentation discipline, source tracking, and the ability to connect ERCOT rule changes to practical desk-support commentary.
