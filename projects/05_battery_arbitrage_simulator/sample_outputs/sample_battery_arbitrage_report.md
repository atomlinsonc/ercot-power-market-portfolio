# Battery Storage Arbitrage Simulator Report

Mock-data notice: This report uses mock ERCOT-style price data until live public ERCOT/GridStatus data is connected.

## Executive Summary

The sample simulator applies a transparent charge/hold/discharge strategy to hourly real-time prices for one ERCOT settlement point. The goal is to demonstrate the analyst workflow: convert prices into dispatch decisions, track state of charge, calculate gross margin, and explain the assumptions.

## Model Assumptions

- Settlement point: HB_NORTH
- Battery capacity: 100 MWh
- Power rating: 50 MW
- Round-trip efficiency: 86%
- Starting state of charge: 50 MWh
- Charge rule: charge during lower-price intervals
- Discharge rule: discharge during higher-price intervals

## Dispatch Summary

- Charge intervals: generated by the threshold strategy
- Discharge intervals: generated by the threshold strategy
- Gross margin: calculated from mock hourly prices
- Equivalent cycles: discharged MWh divided by available capacity
- Ending state of charge: tracked after each interval

## Highest-Value Intervals

The strongest intervals are the hours where real-time prices are high enough to trigger discharge. In a live-data version, these intervals would be compared against DA/RT spreads, load forecast error, reserve conditions, and potential congestion context.

## Items to Watch

- Whether storage value is concentrated in a few scarcity-like hours.
- Whether the selected settlement point consistently produces wider price separation.
- Whether state-of-charge constraints prevent the battery from capturing later high-price intervals.
- Whether adding degradation costs materially changes the result.

## Questions for Further Analysis

- How would results change by hub or load zone?
- What would a perfect-foresight dispatch benchmark produce?
- How should end-of-day state of charge be handled?
- Could ancillary service revenue be modeled after the relevant ERCOT market rules are reviewed?

## Analyst Takeaway

This project is useful because it connects market prices to asset-optimization questions while keeping assumptions visible. The next improvement is to connect live ERCOT/GridStatus data and add a benchmark optimization model.
